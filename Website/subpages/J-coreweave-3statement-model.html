CoreWeave, Inc. (CRWV) — Investment Summary
US GAAP | Units: $M | NASDAQ: CRWV | Sector: Cloud Infrastructure / GPU-as-a-Service
1. BUSINESS PROFILE & INVESTMENT THESIS
Core Business: Purpose-built GPU cloud infrastructure provider (Neocloud). Operates 28 data centers across NA/EU with ~600K GPUs.
Revenue Model: Take-or-pay contracts with hyperscalers (Microsoft ~62% of FY2025 rev), AI labs, and enterprises. Multi-year committed arrangements with 3-5yr weighted avg contract life.
Key Assets: NVIDIA H100/H200/GB200 GPU fleet, proprietary Kubernetes orchestration (CoreWeave Cloud OS), 850MW+ active power capacity.
Moat: (1) First-mover GPU cloud scale; (2) Deep NVIDIA partnership (preferred partner status); (3) Purpose-built infra vs retrofitted hyperscaler; (4) Take-or-pay revenue visibility ($60.7B RPO, $87.8B backlog).
INVESTMENT THESIS — Asymmetric Risk-Reward; Wait for Catalyst
Core View: CoreWeave is a high-quality GPU infrastructure franchise with $88B backlog and 60% EBITDA margin — but the market is pricing this correctly at $118. Probability-weighted target ~$139 (+17% upside) against 35% Bear+Distress probability (-60% to -100%). This is a 3-year growth trade, not a long-term hold: moat decays from 4/5 to 2/5 over 5 years as GPU scarcity fades, contracts reprice, and inference switching costs stay low.
What Must Go Right: (1) Revenue visibility — $60.7B RPO locks ~$13B/yr through 2027, but 2030+ drops to ~$3.3B/yr without $20-25B/yr new backlog; (2) EBITDA scales from $3.1B to $7-11B (FY26-27E) enabling deleverage from 5.65x to <3.5x; (3) GAAP profitability by FY2027E requires $19-20B revenue — narrow margin of error; (4) FCF turns positive by FY2028-29 as capex decelerates from $20B to $10B.
What Can Go Wrong: (1) Customer concentration — MSFT 67% of revenue, top-3 = 77-89% of backlog; systemic retrenchment is existential; (2) GPU oversupply 2027-28 — TSMC CoWoS doubling + $400B+ hyperscaler capex + 4 custom silicon programs converge; H100 pricing already crashed 64%; (3) Leverage — 5.65x net leverage with 0.35x covenant headroom; FY2026 H2 still needs $8-12B funding; CDS 665bps implies 42% 5yr default probability.
Decision Framework: Q1 FY2026 margin recovery (May earnings) = increase position. Q1 margin miss = reduce. Covenant headroom <0.15x = exit immediately. Position sizing: 3-5% for high-risk growth, 1-2% balanced, 0-0.5% conservative. Capital-intensive D&A-heavy model caps mature valuation at AerCap 6-8x (not Equinix 20x).
Scenario Summary (FY2027E): Bull $326 (20%) | Base $155 (40%) | Bear $47 (25%) | Distress $0 (10%). Weighted ~$139 (+17%). 12-month discounted at 15% CoE: ~$121, aligning with analyst consensus $120-125.

7. MARKET & COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
TAM: Global GPU cloud infrastructure market estimated at $50-80B (2025), growing 35-45% CAGR through 2030 (sources: IDC, Gartner, Goldman Sachs).
Market Structure: Oligopolistic — dominated by hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP) with emerging neocloud segment (CoreWeave, Lambda, Together AI).
Competitive Positioning:
Hyperscalers (AWS/Azure/GCP): Broadest cloud portfolio; GPU as one of many services. Higher prices but integrated ecosystem.
CoreWeave: Pure-play GPU cloud; purpose-built infra; lower cost/GPU-hour; preferred NVIDIA partner. First-mover in dedicated GPU cloud at scale.
Lambda Labs: Smaller GPU cloud (~$400M rev); consumer/researcher focus; less enterprise penetration.
Together AI: AI model training platform; smaller scale; more software-oriented.
On-premise/custom (Meta, xAI): Large AI labs building own GPU clusters; not competing for external workloads.
CoreWeave Moat Assessment (Current → 5yr):
Scale: HIGH → MEDIUM — 600K GPUs, 28 DCs, 850MW. Largest independent GPU cloud today; but Nebius (2GW contracted), Crusoe (4.5GW) scaling fast.
NVIDIA Partnership: HIGH → LOW — Preferred partner + $2B equity, but non-exclusive ($2B in Nebius too). TSMC CoWoS doubling removes packaging bottleneck; custom silicon (Maia, TPU, Trainium) erodes NVIDIA centrality.
Switching Costs: MEDIUM → LOW — Training workloads (high friction, TB checkpoints) vs inference (stateless, multi-cloud trivial). As inference rises from 33% to 67%+ of TAM, aggregate switching cost declines.
IP/Software: MEDIUM → MEDIUM — W&B ($1.7B), storage ARR >$100M, CoreWeave Cloud OS. Only moat dimension that improves over time; ecosystem integration is the hedge against commoditization.
Weighted Composite: 4/5 (current) → 2/5 (5yr). All pricing sources except ecosystem integration are time-decaying assets. This is a 3-year growth trade — returns must be captured before moat erodes.
Key Competitive Threats:
Hyperscaler GPU price compression — margin erosion if AWS/Azure match CoreWeave pricing
Custom silicon (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium/Inferentia) — reduces NVIDIA dependency for some workloads
Commoditization of GPU compute — as supply scales, differentiation shifts to software/orchestration layer
